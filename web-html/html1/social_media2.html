1. FACEBOOK

Facebook is one of the largest social media platforms, launched in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg. It allows users to connect with friends and family, share posts, photos, and videos, and join groups based on interests. Facebook also offers features like Marketplace for buying and selling, live streaming, and Stories for temporary updates. Businesses and influencers use it for marketing through pages, ads, and engagement tools. With billions of active users worldwide, Facebook remains a major platform for social networking, communication, and online communities.

2. INSTAGRAM

Instagram is a popular social media platform focused on photo and video sharing. Launched in 2010 and later acquired by Facebook (now Meta), it allows users to post images, reels, and stories that disappear after 24 hours. Instagram is widely used by influencers, brands, and creators for content marketing and engagement. Features like filters, hashtags, and IG Live help users connect and grow their audience. With millions of active users worldwide, Instagram remains a leading platform for visual storytelling, social interactions, and digital trends.
